Your Chiropractic Questions Answered

What is a chiropractor?

Chiropractors help improve quality of life by providing effective pain relief. With their spine, muscles, and nervous system expertise, chiropractors specialize in diagnosing, treating, and creating customized care plans. Ultimately, their goal is to help you stay mobile and pain-free.

What conditions can a chiropractor treat?

Chiropractors provide physical therapy, such as massages or motion exercises, depending on your needs and areas of pain, to readjust the musculoskeletal system.

Chiropractors commonly treat back pain, but they can also address other conditions, such as:

  • Bad posture
  • Headaches
  • Joint or muscle pain, including arthritis
  • Sprains, strains, or sports and occupational injuries
  • Disc bulge

 

Chiropractors use hands-on and non-invasive techniques, such as spinal adjustments, mobilizations, and therapeutic exercises, to relieve pain, improve mobility, and enhance the body’s ability to function.

How much does a chiropractor cost and do I need a referral?

You do not need a referral to visit a chiropractor. But, like many allied health professionals, provincial health insurance may not cover your appointment.

However, if you have private health insurance, chiropractic care is often covered. Its a good idea to check your provincial and private plans for more information.

What would your chiropractor appointment involve? And what should you wear to the chiropractor?

During a chiropractor appointment, you can expect a comprehensive health history evaluation, a physical examination, and a musculoskeletal system assessment.

Chiropractors may perform spinal adjustments or other manual therapies to address any identified issues. It’s recommended to wear comfortable clothing that allows for ease of movement, such as athletic wear or loose-fitting garments. This ensures the chiropractor can easily assess and treat your condition effectively.
